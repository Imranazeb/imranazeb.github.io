## Analysis
Aehr Test Systems is a California-based technology company specializing in semiconductor test and burn-in equipment, a niche but critical segment of the broader semiconductor supply chain. The company's systems are designed to test and weed out defective chips before they reach end products, reducing costly failures down the line. As the demand for high-reliability chips grows across electric vehicles, 5G infrastructure, and AI hardware, companies like Aehr occupy an increasingly vital role.

The semiconductor equipment and materials industry has been experiencing a renaissance, driven by massive global investments in chip manufacturing capacity. Governments and private enterprises worldwide are pouring billions into new fabrication plants, and that means demand for the specialized testing equipment that Aehr provides is on a long-term upward trajectory. Today's sharp move may reflect renewed confidence that Aehr is well-positioned to capitalize on these structural tailwinds.

Aehr has previously been a favorite among investors bullish on the silicon carbide (SiC) semiconductor market, which is booming thanks to electric vehicle adoption. SiC chips require rigorous testing due to their high-performance applications, making Aehr's wafer-level burn-in technology particularly well-suited to the task. Any positive news related to SiC demand or new customer wins can have an outsized effect on a company of Aehr's size.

It's worth noting that smaller-cap technology companies like Aehr can experience amplified price swings compared to large-cap peers.
